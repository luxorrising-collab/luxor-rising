A local's orientation

How Luxor actually splits up

The west bank

Where the dead were buried and the memorial temples stand — the Valley of the Kings, Medinet Habu, Hatshepsut, the Colossi. This is the half most people underestimate, and the half we love most. Go early; it's exposed and hot by midday.

The east bank

The living city, and the two great temples — Karnak and Luxor Temple, joined by the reopened Avenue of the Sphinxes. Karnak at opening, before the coaches, is a different place entirely.

Best time to visit

October to April is comfortable; December and January are busiest. The shoulder months — November, February, March — are the quiet sweet spot. Whenever you come, the trick isn't the month, it's the hour: dawn beats everything.

Luxor after dark

Luxor Temple lit at night, the Corniche, and the souk away from the tourist stalls. A quiet, local evening is worth planning — especially the night before a dawn start.
